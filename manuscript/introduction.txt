## Introduction — Final Nature Medicine version

### Paragraph 1 — Disease + unresolved problem (~140 words)

Multiple sclerosis (MS) is one of the most common causes of non-traumatic neurological disability in young adults, affecting approximately 2.8 million people worldwide¹,². Patients diagnosed under identical criteria follow divergent disability trajectories and respond unequally to the same disease-modifying therapies, and current course descriptors do not reflect the underlying biological heterogeneity of the disease³. A growing body of evidence places the biological onset of MS 5 to 15 years before the first clinical event⁴,⁵,⁶,⁷, during which neuroaxonal injury, autoantibody generation, and non-specific prodromal symptoms emerge⁷,⁸,⁹. However, the leading pre-diagnostic blood biomarker, serum neurofilament light chain, is non-specifically elevated across multiple neurological conditions¹⁰, limiting its utility for MS-specific risk stratification. Whether the pre-symptomatic stage harbors biologically coherent subtypes that could inform targeted prevention remains unknown.

### Paragraph 2 — What's been done + the integrative gap (~115 words)

MS susceptibility is shaped by the HLA-DRB1\*15:01 allele and more than 230 additional risk variants that together explain approximately 40% of heritability¹¹, by Epstein-Barr virus infection as a necessary cause¹², and by modifiable exposures including obesity, low vitamin D, and smoking¹³. Plasma proteomic changes preceding clinical MS onset have been described in non-representative cohorts⁷, and peripheral blood immunological endophenotypes have been established in individuals with established disease¹⁴. However, no study has integrated genetic susceptibility, pre-diagnostic phenomic exposure, and plasma proteomic signatures to define discrete MS subtypes before the first clinical event.

### Paragraph 3 — What we did + the sequential questions (~135 words)

In this study, we integrate genotype, pre-diagnostic phenomic profiles, and plasma Olink proteomics across 125 pre-onset and 113 post-onset MS cases with 47,801 matched controls from UK Biobank. We first ask whether a pre-symptomatic MS plasma proteomic signature is detectable at population scale and which biological pathways it implicates. We next delineate the temporal evolution of central nervous system and immune proteins across the window surrounding diagnosis. We then stratify the pre-symptomatic proteome by HLA-DRB1\*15:01 carrier status and MS polygenic risk to determine how genetic susceptibility shapes pre-clinical biology. We further examine sex differences in a cohort reflective of the true MS sex distribution. Finally, we use pre-diagnostic phenomic clustering to test whether MS exhibits discrete endophenotypes with distinct plasma proteomic biology before the first clinical event.
